# Transcript Intelligence — Take-Home Assignment

## Background

You are working with a B2B enterprise SaaS company that captures call transcripts from across the organization, including:

- **Customer Support Calls** — customers reporting issues or requesting help
- **External Customer Calls** — account managers discussing renewals, adoption, feedback, and relationship management
- **Internal Calls** — engineering syncs, escalations, planning discussions, and cross-functional coordination

The company is building a product called **Transcript Intelligence** — a system that helps stakeholders across the business make better decisions using transcript data.

Potential users include:

- Support leaders
- Sales managers
- Product managers
- Engineering leads

You will receive approximately **100 sample transcripts** across the different call types as your initial dataset.

Your objective is to explore the dataset, extract meaningful insights, and demonstrate your analytical and technical thinking.

---

# Objectives

## 1. Build a Transcript Processing & Categorization Pipeline

Create a pipeline that processes transcripts and categorizes them into meaningful topics or themes.

Your solution should include:

- The categories/themes you identified
- Why those categories matter
- The methodology you chose:
  - LLM-based
  - Clustering
  - Rule-based
  - Hybrid approach
  - Or another approach of your choice
- A clear explanation of your reasoning and tradeoffs
- Example transcripts or excerpts for each category
- How you evaluated or validated the quality of categorization

Focus on demonstrating your thought process, not just the final output.

---

## 2. Perform Sentiment Analysis & Trend Discovery

Analyze sentiment across the different call types and identify patterns or trends.

Your analysis should include:

- Sentiment distribution by call type
- Notable trends or anomalies
- Interpretation of what the trends may indicate
- Why these insights would matter to business stakeholders

Do not limit the work to charts or metrics — explain the business implications behind the findings.

Examples of useful observations:

- Rising frustration around a specific product area
- Positive adoption signals from enterprise customers
- Escalation-heavy internal discussions tied to operational bottlenecks
- Correlation between sentiment and renewal risk

---

## 3. Identify Additional High-Value Insights

Go beyond the required tasks and identify additional insights that could make the Transcript Intelligence product more valuable.

Think from the perspective of different stakeholders across the organization.

Document at least **2–3 additional insight ideas**, including:

- What the insight is
- Who would benefit from it
- Why it matters
- How it could be implemented

You may implement these ideas or describe them conceptually with strong reasoning.

Potential directions include (but are not limited to):

- Churn or renewal risk detection
- Feature request clustering
- Escalation prediction
- Customer health scoring
- Product adoption signals
- Internal operational bottlenecks
- Competitive intelligence extraction
- Action item extraction
- Root cause trend analysis

---

# Deliverables

Provide:

- Your code and/or notebooks
- Documentation of your approach
- Key findings and insights
- Assumptions and tradeoffs
- Visualizations where useful
- Clear explanations of methodology and reasoning

The emphasis is on:

- Analytical thinking
- Product intuition
- Technical decision-making
- Communication clarity
- Insight quality

Not just model accuracy or polished dashboards.
